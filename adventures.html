Shooting For The First Time

2/6/2016

0 Comments

 
2 Weeks ago, me and my roommates went to a shooting range. I have never shot any firearms until then. I was quite curious about how do they feel and sound in real life. The only information I had about firearms was limited with Call of Duty and so on.  In Turkey (my home country), we have gun control, therefore civilians can't access to assault weapons like in U.S. 

I shot with G36, AK47 and Vector. I expected strong recoil on my shoulder but interestingly nearly had none compared to what I expected. However when you fire in burst mode, the rifle tends to go up quite fast. The rifles were extremely loud. Even with the ear protection It was quite loud. The shooting range was indoors because of that the sound was even more amplified. It was quite tiring.  Aiming with iron sights is also not that easy. The rifles were not maintained well anyways, so I can't comment on the accuracy that much. 

After the range I felt a little sick. There are some people who have to use that weapons to stay alive or hurt someone else. The main purpose of a fire arm is to hurt someone really bad. I felt disturbed after using such "tool" even though we were just shooting at a range not hunting. Simultaneously there are quite a lot of people in Iraq Syria region (ISIS, daesh or whatever they are) who are not identifiable as humans.  M. K. Ataturk (founder of the Republic of Turkey) said that "Unless a nation's life faces peril, war is murder."  ​

U.S. Adventure & Georgia Tech

8/12/2015

0 Comments

 
I just arrived to Georgia Tech, I had a 16 hour flight and 3 planes..... I flew with Turkish Airlines from Istanbul to New York, spent 2 hours getting through the customs.  After that, I flew to Charlotte and then Atlanta.
The picture above is the Charlotte airport, which was really fancy!
This is a road in Atlanta, all green smelled really nice and fresh.
Houses were like this around the place that I stayed. They look utopic. The life in United States (as long as I experienced) is really similar to the ones in the films. With everything, the ugly and the nice. The life standards are pretty high compared to Istanbul. There is no air polution, the streets are pretty organized, people care about their jobs and duties and they are well paid. I'm aware of the facts that all of this requires quite lot of resources but as long as I experienced this is how humans should live in 21st ceuntry... Not battling with traffic, not dealing with riots of minorities, without ridicilous religious conflicts. People are quite friendly in here, however I sensed that people felt strange when I said I was Turkish.. Which is a bit sad... 
This is a street at Georgia Tech, Its all organized clean, warm and humid. We have free bus rides all around the campus, and at night we have something called stingerrette to take you around safely.
This is our library building which has a roof garden. Tomorrow I'm going to visit the invention studio and see the Maker Space and such.
at Georgia Tech, we have like three giant swimming pools and they are HUGE, maan just look at that! We can use all of them.

OUT OF CIVILIZATION

11/16/2014

0 Comments

 
We(with Macit, Alara, Asli)  decided to go camping outside of istanbul . We packed up tents, sleeping bags, my ATV. Macit's jeep was suitable for our trip. 
We went like 60km outside of Istanbul the views were amazing. I won't tell the location because otherwise our place would get poluted with migros bags and all kind of stuff. People dont know how to clean up after they leave somewhere. The place where we used to go, an ancient dam behind bahcesehir, literally got "raped". Not because that I shared the location but many people saw us going there, especially the locals with low-education.
We arrived this place at night where the only night source was moon. I felt like I was in ancient rome. It was wonderful being able to see in dark without any artificial instruments. It was a utopic feeling. I felt so different and promised myself to bring my girlfriend there at summer. The air was a bit cold, It would be splendid at a full moon summer night!
This beach goes on and on, experienced campers would be able to understand where were we. 
We had an ATV, and Macit's Jeep. You cant drive on the sand if you dont have an offroad vehicle, because it sinks.
Things I hated: 

Some genious insisted that we shouldn't sleep because this is a great night, we should have fun till the morning. I saw her suffer 2 hours later because of the cold :) ,not smart. I comfortably slept at the tent. 

Air was a bit cold

Sand got into our shoes a lot

No place to sit! 

Anyways It was great but not again till summer.
