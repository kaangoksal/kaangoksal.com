Drone Workshop ıN A Mıddle School

3/1/2016

0 Comments

 
I always wanted to try teaching younglings how to build a drone. I started making drones when I was in 8th grade, however due to lack of knowledge and skills, the drone I was working with crashed quite frequently. Most of the time I didn't know the reason. I spent 2 years to make it fly. Luckly these kids have a mentor like me so they will be able to fly the drones in couple of weeks. If something goes wrong I'll be there with them to "help" with the troubleshooting. Troubleshooting is an essential skill for STEM fields. I'm not planning to spoon feed them! 

​
I'm currently a student at Georgia Tech, before coming to U.S. I was in Robert College. Due to my unique work in Turkey (the startup, makers club and so) I had the opportunity to give speeches in Universities and conferences. I eventually met some professors located in Atlanta! After I came here, they introduced me to Lovett School. I mentioned them I'm flying & building drones. Middle School was interested in the project, so we planned the curriculum and the items that we are going to buy for at least 6 weeks. 

​
I want to talk about the lectures:

In order to be an effective lecturer, I have to prepare lots of stuff before hand. If I'm going to teach for 1 hour class, I have to prepare for at least 1 hour. 

If I really want to get the attention of the kids, I need to pull all of them in to the course by getting them busy. Either I have to give them something to think about: A Quiz or something that they can apply using their hands: Like assembling stuff. I didn't give them anything that would challenge their math, skills or require them heavy thinking - yet :)))))))). So we will see whether they will stay focused on such challenge.

Things I'm satisfied with: 

The kids are quite skillful, I would expect them to have harder time with screws and wiring, but they didn't! 

If I plan the lecture before hand, Everything goes smooth. I have to plan for each individual student about what they are going to do, and what items they need to have in their boxes. If one student has nothing to do but watch the other group members, he starts to get distracted. 

Things I don't like: 

It is quite tiring, I can't believe the energy that these kids have... 

Sometimes kids are really loud, so I have to come up with competitions about which group is going to be the quietest and most organized one. I provide additional tips and items to the quiet groups so they progress easier and faster. I have suspicions about whether this is a right method to motivate students to be quiet/organized.  But it works! 

Things I'm worried about:

I'm worried about geofencing, I will have to code some stuff into the drones to limit their flight abilities. We don't really want drones flying away. I will have to test it before hand.

I'm also worried about drones colliding on air. I have no efficient solution to manage the drones in a small airspace. We are talking about a space like 20meters to 10 meters and there will be 4 drones airborne. The GPS accuracy is like 1.4m so, Eventhoug I write an algorithm to the ground stations to check the other drones It would be quite time consuming... and I don't know how effective is it going to be.

Drones

9/22/2015

0 Comments

 
In my senior year, I had the opportunity to work with drones. I designed, programmed and integrated hardware to create different quadrocopters with various endurances and payload capabilities. The render on the right is  a 3D Printed drone which has a foldable frame. It has a range of 5 miles and It can operate on 3G network, you can watch the first person view from the drone and get the gps location and all other details without no reception issues!
My journey with drones started with this black frame which I bought online. When I started to design my own frames from scratch I faced many issues, ranging from vibrations, to electromagnetic interference. 

The first prototypes consistent of single board for flight control, navigation and digital communication over 433mhz. The video transmission from the drone was anolog 1.2 mhz. We didnt use any encryption.



The second prototype, included 2 boards, one for flight control and the second one for communications and navigation. Also in the second iteration, the telemetry link was encrypted and used 3g to communicate. 
The drone in this video weights 5 kilograms, It can fly upto 55 minutes and has a theoratical range of 14km (we tested maximum of 10km), It has a thermal camera and a day camera which is capable of 30x optical zoom. This drone has two telemetry options, 3G, and radio  telemetry. Both comms are encrypted and can operate up to range of 20kms. I can't disclose any further details yet since it is an ongoing project.
Overall I have a total of 158.7 hours of flight. 94 hours autonomous and 64 hours manual. The longest distance that I piloted a drone was over 11km. I had many crashes and spent many hours on crash log analysis. My biggest issues were so far 1. Vibration & resonance 2. Electromagnetic interference.

Connecthing

9/18/2015

3 Comments

 
Connecthing is my first startup. We founded connecthing as 4 people. We all met thanks to Hydrogen Powered Car Club at Yıldız Technical University. The picture on the right is me and Ilkay at Ankara. We were about to pitch our project to ministry of industry and technology to get funding.
This is our first product, Think Thing Wire, It is a smart plug that connects to internet via Wi-fi, It has in dustry level energy sensors, that can measure the properties of the sin waves and so. It can tell you how much energy you consume in terms of currency and It can detect standby energy. The best part of this product is, It can reduce your energy bill. In Turkey we have dynamic energy pricing where the price of electric is 47cents before ten o'clock and 11cents after ten o'clock. When this product is connected to a dishwasher and the dishwasher is turned on at 6pm, this plug sends a notification to your phone that says right now the energy price is 47cents but after ten o'clock, it is 47 cents. Woud you like to wash your dishes for the one third of the price? If you press OK, Think Thing Wire turns off your dishwasher and turns it back on again when energy is cheaper. 
This is TT Bridge, It connects your IR suppoerted devices to internet. This way you can interact with your house wherever you like!
This photograph is from Turkcell Technology Summit where I pitched connecting.
This is the PCB of the Think Thing wire, It is designed by Ilkay, we had lots of iterations due to problems with electromagnetic interference. 

Product Development Process
The first design of Think Thing wire is on the right. It was just round. It could easily contain the pcb. The green circle around the plug is a rgb led strip. We were planning to change the color of the led acording to spesific events such as current, on, off.
This was our second design. It was smaller in diameter and volume, however we were not satisfied with the visual of the product. It looked simple, not fancy. 
This our 3rd design, It looks quite similar to nest thermostat. The led strip is again visible around the plug. 
After 3 attemts of design we decided to work with an industrial designer. We supplied him with the sci-fi pictures and fictional designs. That was more than enough. He just came up with this and also many other designs. However this design need some improvements. We need certification for this plug to be used on AC Plugs, therefore it has to follow some guidelines. The render at the bottom represents the design which would pass the certification.

Thıng Thınk Brıdge

This is Think Thing Bridge. I came up with this product. What it does is simple but yet smart. Bridge helps you to get rid of all your ir remotes and turns your IR supported appliances into IoT devices. With this device you can just use your smartphone to interact with your TV, music system, air conditioning and many other devices. Its not only that. This device also helps your old infrared ac to be integrated to your smart house system. You can turn the air condioner on and off from your office or wherever you are.  This is a portable and a quick solution for old devices to convert them into smart home appliances.
This device is connected to internet via Wi-fi, It has an infrared receiver and a transmitter and a battery that lasts about a year. You can charge it with micro-usb. It has also a bluetooth 4.0 to be used in future updates.  The industrial design process with this product was easier since I had experience from my previous work.
Our angel investor wanted to sell this product to middle east.

Private Schools COnference

3/1/2015

0 Comments

 
I was invited to the annual private schools conference, where administrators and teachers of private schools come together and discuss their methods in education and future of education. This year's topic was the future teachers. I met Nabi Avcı, who is the minister of education. His speech was well written and smart, there were some messages. He is clearly  a smart and intellectual person. Anyways, the conference was focused on classrooms and students however nearly no one said anything solid about how they are going to improve the quality,  It was mainly abstract. I also noticed there were no other students execpt me and Orkun, It is ironic that the main focus of education, students, were absent in such conference. 
I and Orkun had a small panel, regarding to how we teach in our clubs, It was fun and instructive. I took pleasure talking infront of teachers. 

What I learned from the conference?
The education system needs to be revised, the main focus of the schools in Turkey is earning money rather than giving high quality creativity oriented education. The education system we have now was designed in 18th ceuntry acording to the industrial revolution where they needed workers for business and companies. We are grouped like Science & Math Students, Literature & Language Students, Art students; after that we are labeled with our production date, like 2015 graduates. I'm not even talking about the examination system where we have only 5 choices, yeah right we will always have 5 choices in life... 
 
I was able to attend this conference because I rejected the idea of having 5 choices, instead I created my choices and founded a company, clubs, did projects that I desired. I read books, I spent sleepless nights in front of monitors to learn programming by myself. It is really fascinating that I was rejected to take programming in my senior year, the teacher said "you couldn't competete with the other students who took the exam" after 5 months I was a co-founder of an Iot Startup and a teacher of multiple programming languages.  Life it is...

(coke and chocolate) How do the people react to the prices, which areas buy which products more often.. and such. The possibilities are limitless.
